Advanced Writing Techniques

Level: C1 Writing
0 / 11 points

1. Rhetorical Devices

Rhetorical devices are techniques that skilled writers use to make their writing more persuasive, memorable, and elegant. Recognising and using them marks a C1 writer.

DeviceDefinitionExample
AnaphoraRepeating a word/phrase at the start of successive clauses"We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds, we shall fight in the fields…"
AntithesisContrasting ideas in parallel structure"It was the best of times, it was the worst of times."
Rhetorical questionA question asked for effect, not a real answer"How much longer can we ignore this crisis?"
TricolonA series of three parallel elements"Life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness."
UnderstatementDeliberately minimising something for effect"The earthquake caused a few minor inconveniences."

2. Cohesion & Sophisticated Transitions

Advanced writers don't just connect ideas — they signal the precise logical relationship between them. This creates writing that feels reasoned, not just assembled.

Beyond Basic Connectors

BasicSophisticated UpgradeFunction
butnonetheless / nevertheless / notwithstanding thisContrast
soconsequently / it follows that / as a corollaryResult
alsofurthermore / what is more / by the same tokenAddition
for exampleto illustrate / this is exemplified by / a case in point isExemplification
in conclusiontaken together / on balance / all things consideredSummary
Weak: "The project failed. But the team learned a lot."
Strong: "The project failed to meet its targets; nevertheless, the experience yielded valuable insights that have since informed subsequent initiatives."

3. Nominalisation & Academic Register

Nominalisation is the process of turning verbs or adjectives into nouns. It is a hallmark of academic and professional writing, making sentences more formal and information-dense.

Verb/AdjectiveNominalised FormExample
decide → decisionThe decision was made to restructure.
analyse → analysisAn in-depth analysis was conducted.
improve → improvementSignificant improvement was observed.
significant → significanceThe significance of the findings…
conclude → conclusionThe conclusion drawn was…
Everyday: "They decided to investigate why sales dropped."
Academic: "A decision was made to conduct an investigation into the decline in sales figures."
