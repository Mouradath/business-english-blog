Email Writing Skills

Level: B1 Writing
0 / 11 points

1. Email Structure & Tone

A professional email has a clear structure. Whether writing formally or informally, the layout remains consistent. The key difference is your choice of language and tone.

PartFormalInformal
Subject lineMeeting Request – Q3 ReviewQuick question about tomorrow
GreetingDear Mr Thompson,Hi Sarah,
OpeningI am writing to enquire about…Just wanted to ask…
BodyI would be grateful if you could…Could you help me with…?
ClosingI look forward to hearing from you.Let me know what you think!
Sign-offYours sincerely, / Kind regards,Thanks, / Best,

2. Subject Lines & Openings

Your subject line is the first thing a reader sees. A vague subject line often means the email is ignored or delayed.

Effective Subject Lines

Weak ❌Strong ✅
HelloFollow-up: Invoice #4521 Due Friday
QuestionQuestion About Your Availability – 15 June
Important!!!Action Required: Contract Review by EOD

Opening Lines

Formal:
"I am writing with regard to…"
"Further to our conversation, I would like to…"
"I hope this email finds you well."

Informal:
"Hope you're doing well!"
"Just following up on…"
"Quick question for you —"

3. Making Requests & Closing Professionally

Requests in professional emails should be polite but clear. Use modal verbs to soften the tone.

Direct (Too blunt)Polite (Better)
Send me the report.Could you please send me the report?
I need an answer.I would appreciate your response by Friday.
Do this immediately.I would be grateful if you could action this at your earliest convenience.

Professional Closings

Sign-offContext
Yours sincerelyFormal (when you know the name)
Yours faithfullyFormal (when you don't know the name)
Kind regardsSemi-formal (most common)
Best regardsSemi-formal to informal
Thanks / CheersInformal only
