Style & Clarity in Writing

Level: B2 Writing
0 / 11 points

1. The Principles of Clear Writing

Clear writing is not about using impressive vocabulary — it's about communicating your ideas as efficiently as possible. The best writers often use the simplest language.

PrincipleWhat it means
EconomyCut every word that doesn't earn its place
PrecisionChoose the exact word, not an approximate one
Active voicePrefer "She wrote the report" over "The report was written"
Varied sentencesMix short punchy sentences with longer flowing ones
Concrete detailsShow, don't tell — use facts, numbers, examples

2. Active vs Passive Voice

Active voice is more direct and energetic. Passive voice can seem evasive or bureaucratic. Knowing when to use each is a sign of a mature writer.

Active ✅Passive (often weaker)
The manager approved the budget.The budget was approved by the manager.
Scientists discovered a new planet.A new planet was discovered.
We made three key decisions.Three key decisions were made.
When passive IS appropriate:
— When the agent is unknown: "The window was broken."
— In scientific writing: "The samples were tested at 37°C."
— To avoid blame: "Mistakes were made." (classic political language)

3. Cutting Wordiness & Improving Precision

Wordy writing exhausts readers. Learn to recognise common filler phrases and replace them with concise alternatives.

Wordy ❌Concise ✅
due to the fact thatbecause
in the event thatif
at this point in timenow
make a decisiondecide
in spite of the fact thatalthough
in order toto

Choosing Precise Words

Vague: "The results were good."
Precise: "The results exceeded targets by 22%."

Vague: "She said something about the deadline."
Precise: "She warned that the deadline was non-negotiable."
