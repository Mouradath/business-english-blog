Essay Writing & Argumentation

Level: B1/B2 Writing
0 / 11 points

1. Essay Structure: Introduction, Body & Conclusion

A well-structured essay guides the reader from the introduction through your argument to a clear conclusion. Think of it as a map — the reader always knows where they are.

PartPurposeLength
IntroductionHook + background + thesis statement~10–15%
Body paragraph 1First main argument + evidence~25–30%
Body paragraph 2Second main argument + evidence~25–30%
Body paragraph 3Counter-argument + rebuttal (optional)~15–20%
ConclusionRestate thesis + summarise + final thought~10–15%

2. Writing a Strong Thesis Statement

The thesis statement is the heart of your essay. It appears at the end of the introduction and tells the reader exactly what you will argue.

Weak thesis: "Social media is popular." (too vague, no argument)

Strong thesis: "Although social media connects people globally, its negative effects on mental health and political discourse outweigh its benefits."

The PEE Paragraph Method

Use P-E-E for each body paragraph to keep your argument focused and well-supported:

LetterMeaningExample
PPoint — your argument"Remote work increases productivity."
EEvidence — data or example"A Stanford study found a 13% rise in output."
EExplanation — why it matters"This shows that flexible environments boost focus."

3. Hedging Language & Counter-Arguments

Hedging means softening your claims so they sound academic and careful rather than overconfident. It's essential for B2+ academic writing.

Hedging Phrases

Too StrongHedged Version
Social media causes depression.Social media may contribute to depression.
This proves that…This suggests / indicates that…
All students struggle.Many students tend to struggle.
The results show…The results appear to show…

Addressing Counter-Arguments

Formula: "While some argue that [counter], it can be seen that [your position] because [evidence]."

Example: "While some argue that social media is an essential communication tool, it can be seen that the psychological costs — particularly for young users — significantly outweigh these benefits."
