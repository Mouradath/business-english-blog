Business Vocabulary Essentials

Level: A2/B1 Business English
0 / 11 points

1. Core Business Nouns

Every professional needs a solid base of business nouns — the names of people, documents, and processes that appear in any workplace.

WordMeaningExample
agendalist of topics for a meetingPlease review the agenda before the meeting.
invoicebill sent to a client for paymentWe sent the invoice on Friday.
deadlinethe latest time to finish somethingThe deadline is next Monday.
revenueincome earned by a businessRevenue increased by 12% this quarter.
stakeholderanyone with an interest in a projectWe need to inform all stakeholders.
budgetplanned spending for a periodWe're over budget this month.
KPIKey Performance Indicator — a measure of successSales volume is our main KPI.

2. Essential Business Verbs

These verbs are the workhorses of professional English. Learning them in context will help you sound natural in meetings, emails, and reports.

VerbMeaningExample sentence
allocateto assign resources to a purposeWe need to allocate more budget to marketing.
delegateto give a task to someone elseGood managers delegate effectively.
forecastto predict future resultsWe forecast 8% growth next year.
streamlineto make a process more efficientWe streamlined our onboarding process.
leverageto use something to maximum advantageWe can leverage our existing network.
liaiseto communicate and cooperatePlease liaise with the logistics team.

3. Business Adjectives & Phrases

These adjectives and set phrases are used constantly in professional contexts — in performance reviews, strategy meetings, and client calls.

Word / PhraseMeaning
cost-effectivegood value for the money spent
scalableable to grow without losing efficiency
viableworkable; likely to succeed
bottom linethe final profit or loss figure; the key point
going forwardfrom now on / in the future
touch baseto make brief contact to check in
bring to the tableto offer skills or value to a situation
In use: "Going forward, we need a more scalable and cost-effective solution. Let's touch base next week to discuss what each team can bring to the table."
