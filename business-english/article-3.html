Negotiation Skills

Level: B1 Business English
0 / 11 points

1. The Language of Proposals & Offers

Good negotiators know how to make proposals, respond to offers, and push back — all while keeping the relationship positive. The key is to sound firm but collaborative.

FunctionPhrases
Making a proposal"What if we…?" / "How about…?" / "We'd like to propose…"
Making an offer"We could offer you…" / "We're prepared to…" / "We can go as far as…"
Accepting"That works for us." / "We can agree to that." / "That sounds fair."
Rejecting politely"I'm afraid that doesn't work for us." / "We'd need a bit more on that."
Asking for time"Could we have some time to consider?" / "Let me take this back to my team."

2. Bargaining & Compromise Language

Negotiation is a two-way process. The best outcomes are win-win — both sides feel they gained something. These phrases help you trade concessions professionally.

Conditional offers (If… then…):
"If you can increase the order, we could reduce the price."
"If we agree on this point, would you be willing to…?"
"Provided you deliver by the 15th, we'll proceed with the full contract."

Softening Rejections

Too blunt ❌Diplomatic ✅
"No, that price is too high.""That's a little higher than we were expecting."
"We can't do that.""That might be challenging for us at this stage."
"Your deadline is impossible.""The timeline would be quite tight — could we explore some flexibility?"

3. Reaching Agreement & Closing

The closing phase of a negotiation is just as important as the opening. You need to confirm what was agreed, assign next steps, and leave on a positive note.

StagePhrases
Summarising agreement"So, to confirm, we've agreed that…" / "Let me recap what we've decided."
Confirming next steps"The next step will be…" / "We'll put this in writing and send it over."
Positive close"I think this works well for both sides." / "I'm glad we could reach an agreement."
Keeping the door open"I look forward to working with you." / "Let's stay in touch on this."
Key tip: Always follow up a verbal negotiation with a written summary — an email confirming the terms. This avoids misunderstandings and creates a record.
