Advanced Business Strategy Language

Level: C1 Business English
0 / 11 points

1. Strategic Frameworks & Their Language

C1-level business English requires fluency with the vocabulary of strategy — the language used in boardrooms, investor calls, and executive communications. These terms appear in annual reports, strategy documents, and leadership conversations.

TermMeaningIn context
value propositionThe unique benefit a product/service offers"Our value proposition centres on speed and reliability."
competitive advantageWhat sets a company apart from rivals"Our competitive advantage lies in our proprietary technology."
market penetrationStrategy to increase share in existing markets"We're pursuing aggressive market penetration in Southeast Asia."
due diligenceInvestigation before a business decision"We need to complete due diligence before the acquisition."
synergyCombined effect greater than individual parts"The merger will create significant operational synergies."
pivotA fundamental change in business strategy"After Q2 results, the board decided to pivot toward SaaS."

2. Influence & Persuasion in Senior Contexts

At C1 level, you need to go beyond presenting facts — you need to persuade, challenge, and influence senior stakeholders while maintaining credibility and relationships.

FunctionAdvanced phrase
Challenging diplomatically"With respect, I'd like to push back on that assumption."
Reframing the issue"Perhaps the question isn't X, but rather whether Y…"
Building consensus"I think we'd all agree that the priority here is…"
Managing risk language"The downside risk here is limited, whereas the upside potential is substantial."
Escalating urgency"The window of opportunity here is narrow — delay carries real cost."
Seeking alignment"Before we proceed, I want to ensure we're fully aligned on the objectives."

3. Crisis Communication & Damage Limitation

How a company or leader communicates in a crisis defines their reputation. C1 professionals need to master the language of accountability, reassurance, and controlled messaging.

The four stages of crisis communication:
1. Acknowledge — "We are aware of the situation and take it very seriously."
2. Contain — "We have immediately taken steps to address the issue."
3. Explain — "Our investigation has identified the root cause as…"
4. Reassure — "We are committed to ensuring this does not happen again."
SituationProfessional language
Admitting error"We acknowledge that our response fell short of the standard expected."
Avoiding blame"This was the result of a complex set of circumstances."
Promising action"We are committed to a full and transparent review."
Protecting reputation"Our track record over 20 years speaks to our core values."
