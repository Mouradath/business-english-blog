Meetings & Discussions

Level: A2/B1 Business English
0 / 11 points

1. Opening & Chairing a Meeting

The person who leads a meeting is called the chair (or chairperson). A well-chaired meeting starts on time, follows the agenda, and ends with clear action points.

StageUseful Phrases
Opening"Let's get started." / "Shall we begin?" / "Thanks everyone for coming."
State purpose"The aim of today's meeting is to…" / "We're here to discuss…"
Introduce agenda"On the agenda today we have…" / "We have three items to cover."
Moving on"Let's move on to the next point." / "Can we turn to item two?"
Closing"To summarise…" / "Let's wrap up." / "I'll send the minutes shortly."

2. Participating in Discussions

In business meetings, it's important to be able to give your opinion, interrupt politely, and ask for clarification — all without sounding rude.

Giving Opinions

"In my view…" / "From my perspective…" / "I'd argue that…"
"As I see it…" / "If you ask me…" / "My feeling is that…"

Interrupting Politely

"Sorry to interrupt, but…" / "If I could just add something here…"
"Can I come in at this point?" / "Before we move on, I'd like to say…"

Asking for Clarification

"Could you clarify what you mean by…?" / "I'm not sure I follow — could you elaborate?"
"Are you saying that…?" / "Just to confirm, do you mean…?"

3. Action Points & Minutes

At the end of every good meeting, there should be clear action points — specific tasks assigned to specific people with deadlines. The minutes are the written record of what was discussed and decided.

TermMeaning
Action pointA task agreed upon, assigned to someone with a deadline
MinutesWritten summary of a meeting's discussions and decisions
AOBAny Other Business — time at the end for unplanned topics
Follow upTo check on progress after the meeting
Take the floorTo begin speaking in a meeting
Typical action point format:
"Action: [Name] will [task] by [date]."
Example: "Action: Sarah will send the revised proposal to the client by 15 June."
