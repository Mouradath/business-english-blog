Stress and Intonation in English

Sound more natural by mastering word stress and sentence rhythm.

← Back to Speaking

Why Stress and Intonation Matter

Two sentences can have the same words but completely different meanings depending on which word is stressed.

Sentence Stress

Content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) are stressed. Function words (articles, prepositions) are usually weak.

I WANT to BUY a NEW PHONE.

(want, buy, new, phone are stressed — I, to, a are weak)

Changing Stress Changes Meaning

I didn't say she stole it. (someone else said it)

I didn't say she stole it. (I never said it)

I didn't say she stole it. (someone else did)

I didn't say she stole it. (maybe she borrowed it)

Intonation Patterns

Rising intonation (↑) — questions and interest

Are you coming? ↑ / Really? ↑

Falling intonation (↓) — statements and wh-questions

I live in London. ↓ / Where do you live? ↓

✓ What You Learned

Stress content words, not function words. Changing which word you stress changes the meaning. Rising intonation signals questions; falling intonation signals statements.
