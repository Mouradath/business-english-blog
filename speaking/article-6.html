📖 1. Opening Your Presentation

The first 30 seconds of a presentation are the most important. A strong opening grabs your audience's attention and tells them exactly what to expect. In English, the standard structure is: greet → introduce yourself → state your topic → outline your structure.

✅ Opening Phrases

• Good morning everyone. My name is Sarah and today I'm going to talk about...
• Thank you for being here. I'd like to start by asking you a question...
• Today I will cover three main points. First... Second... And finally...
• By the end of this presentation, you will understand...

💡
Engagement tip: Start with a surprising fact, a short question, or a bold statement. For example: "Did you know that 75% of people fear public speaking more than death?" This instantly grabs attention and makes your audience want to listen.

📝 2. Signposting — Guiding Your Audience

Signposting means using phrases to guide your audience through your presentation — telling them where you are, where you're going, and when you're moving on. It makes your talk much easier to follow.

PurposePhrase to Use
Moving to next point"Now let's move on to my second point..."
Giving an example"For example..." / "To illustrate this..."
Summarising a point"So, to summarise what I've said so far..."
Referring to a visual"As you can see in this chart..."
Inviting questions"I'd be happy to take any questions now."
Concluding"To conclude, I'd like to highlight three key takeaways..."
✅ Full Mini-Presentation Structure

Open: "Good morning. Today I will talk about three main points."
Point 1: "First, let's look at the current situation..."
Transition: "Now let's move on to my second point..."
Point 2: "As you can see in this chart, the trend is..."
Conclude: "To conclude, the key takeaway is..."
Questions: "Thank you. I'd be happy to take any questions."

🎯
Delivery tip: Speak more slowly than feels natural — nervousness makes people rush. Pause for 2 seconds after each main point. Pausing sounds powerful, not weak. The audience needs time to absorb what you've said.

⚠️ 3. Common Mistakes

These are the most common errors B2 learners make when presenting in English. Study them carefully!

❌ Common Errors

• ❌ "I will speak about..." — Unnatural; use "talk about" or "discuss"
• ❌ "In conclusion, I want to say that in conclusion..." — Never repeat conclusion phrases
• ❌ "Any questions?" — Too abrupt; soften it
• ❌ Reading directly from notes word-for-word — Use bullet points, speak naturally

✅ Correct Forms

• ✅ "Today I'm going to talk about / discuss..."
• ✅ "To conclude, the three key points are..." (say it once, clearly)
• ✅ "Thank you for listening. I'd be happy to take any questions."
• ✅ Use short bullet-point notes and speak naturally from memory
