📖 1. Asking for Directions

When you are lost or need help finding a place, you need to ask someone for directions. In English, it is polite to start with Excuse me before asking.

✅ Useful Phrases — Asking

• Excuse me, how do I get to the station?
• Could you tell me the way to the supermarket?
• Is the bank far from here?
• Where is the nearest bus stop?

💡
Politeness tip: Always start with "Excuse me" when stopping a stranger. It is much more polite than just asking directly. You can also say "Sorry to bother you, but…" to sound very natural.

📝 2. Giving Directions

When someone asks you for directions, use these clear and simple phrases. Use imperatives (command verbs) — they are the most natural way to give directions.

Direction PhraseMeaning
Go straight aheadContinue forward without turning
Turn left / Turn rightChange direction to the left or right
Take the first leftTurn left at the first street
Go past the churchContinue until you pass the church
It's on your left / rightThe place is on that side
It's opposite the parkIt faces the park directly
It's next to the caféIt is beside the café
✅ Full Dialogue Example

A: Excuse me, how do I get to the train station?
B: Sure! Go straight ahead, then turn left at the traffic lights. The station is on your right, next to the supermarket.
A: Thank you so much!
B: No problem, it's about five minutes on foot.

🎯
Speaking tip: When giving directions, use landmarks like "the big red building" or "the café on the corner". This makes your directions much easier to follow!

⚠️ 3. Common Mistakes

These are errors that many learners make when asking for or giving directions. Study them carefully!

❌ Common Errors

• ❌ "Where is the station, please?" — Too abrupt without "Excuse me"
• ❌ "Go at the left" — Wrong preposition
• ❌ "It is in front of the street" — Confusing preposition usage
• ❌ "Take a left on the second road" — Mixing structures awkwardly

✅ Correct Forms

• ✅ "Excuse me, where is the station?"
• ✅ "Turn left" or "Go left"
• ✅ "It is at the end of the street"
• ✅ "Take the second road on the left"
