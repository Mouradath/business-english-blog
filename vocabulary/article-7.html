📖 1. Academic Verbs

Academic writing uses specific verbs that signal your intent clearly. These are called academic verbs and they appear constantly in essays, reports, and research papers.

VerbMeaningExample
analyseexamine in detailThis essay analyses the causes of climate change.
arguepresent a case for somethingThe author argues that inequality is increasing.
demonstrateshow clearly with evidenceThe data demonstrates a clear link.
indicatepoint to / suggestThe results indicate a significant improvement.
evaluatejudge the value or quality ofWe must evaluate the evidence carefully.
concludereach a decision after thinkingThe study concludes that sleep affects performance.
suggestput forward an ideaThe findings suggest a need for further research.
💡
Academic tip: In academic writing, avoid saying "I think" or "I believe". Instead use: "The evidence suggests..." or "The data indicates...". This sounds more objective and professional.

📝 2. Key Academic Nouns

These academic nouns appear in almost every university-level text. Learning them will help you read and write more confidently at B2 level.

✅ Essential Academic Nouns

• hypothesis — an educated guess to be tested: "The hypothesis was confirmed by the experiment."
• evidence — facts that support a claim: "There is strong evidence for this theory."
• argument — a set of reasons supporting a view: "Her argument is well-structured."
• data — facts and statistics collected for analysis: "The data clearly supports the conclusion."
• framework — a structure for understanding something: "We used a theoretical framework."
• assumption — something taken as true without proof: "This argument rests on a false assumption."

🎯
Important: "Data" is technically plural (the singular is "datum"), but in modern English it is commonly used with a singular verb: "The data shows..." or "The data show..." — both are acceptable at B2 level.

⚠️ 3. Common Mistakes

These are the most common academic vocabulary errors at B2 level.

❌ Common Errors

• ❌ "I think the evidence shows..." — too informal for academic writing
• ❌ "The datas show..." — "data" has no plural -s in English
• ❌ "This proves that..." — overconfident; use "suggests" or "indicates"
• ❌ "In my opinion, the hypothesis is right." — avoid personal opinion language

✅ Correct Forms

• ✅ "The evidence suggests that..."
• ✅ "The data indicates a clear trend."
• ✅ "The findings strongly support this hypothesis."
• ✅ "As the analysis demonstrates, the results are significant."
