📖 1. Essential Workplace Vocabulary

Whether you work in an office, from home, or in any professional environment, knowing workplace vocabulary is essential for communicating clearly in English.

WordMeaningExample
deadlinefinal date to finish somethingThe deadline is Friday at 5pm.
colleagueperson you work withMy colleagues are very helpful.
salarymonthly payment for workShe negotiated a higher salary.
promotionmoving to a higher positionHe got a promotion after two years.
overtimeextra hours beyond normal work timeI worked overtime to finish the report.
resignationformally leaving a jobShe handed in her resignation yesterday.
remote workworking from home or elsewhereRemote work has become very common.
appraisalformal review of your performanceMy annual appraisal is next week.
💡
Culture tip: In British workplaces, it is common to give one month's notice before leaving a job. In the US, two weeks is standard. Knowing this difference can be important in professional English conversations!

📝 2. Career Verbs

These verbs describe the key events in a person's working life. Learn them to talk about careers naturally.

✅ Key Career Verbs

• hire / recruit — to give someone a job: "The company hired 20 new staff."
• promote — to give a higher position: "She was promoted to team leader."
• resign — to leave your job voluntarily: "He resigned after ten years."
• retire — to stop working permanently (usually at old age): "She retired at 65."
• dismiss / fire / sack — to force someone to leave: "He was dismissed for misconduct."
• apply for — to formally request a job: "She applied for three positions."

✅ Talking About Work

• "What do you do for a living?" — asking about someone's job
• "I work for a tech company." — saying your employer
• "I'm in charge of the marketing team." — describing your role
• "I'm looking for a new opportunity." — polite way to say job-hunting

🎯
Key difference: "resign" vs "retire" — you resign at any age when you choose to leave. You retire when you permanently stop working, usually at retirement age. Don't confuse them!

⚠️ 3. Common Mistakes

These are the most common work vocabulary errors at B1 level.

❌ Common Errors

• ❌ "She retired from her job to work elsewhere." — retire means stop working permanently
• ❌ "He was promoted a manager." — use "promoted to": "promoted to manager"
• ❌ "I work in a big salary." — say "I earn a good salary"
• ❌ "My boss gave me a raise of salary." — say "a pay rise" (UK) or "a raise" (US)

✅ Correct Forms

• ✅ "She retired after 30 years in education."
• ✅ "He was promoted to manager last month."
• ✅ "I earn a competitive salary."
• ✅ "My boss gave me a pay rise."
