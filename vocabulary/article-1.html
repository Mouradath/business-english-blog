📖 1. The Most Essential English Verbs

Verbs are the engine of every sentence — they express actions, states, and events. As a beginner, learning the most common verbs will unlock your ability to communicate in hundreds of everyday situations.

VerbMeaningExample
beto exist / equalI am a student.
haveto possessShe has a cat.
doto performI do my homework.
goto move / travelWe go to school.
getto receive / obtainHe gets good grades.
maketo create / produceShe makes coffee.
knowto have knowledgeI know this word.
thinkto have a thoughtI think it's correct.
wantto desireThey want to learn.
useto employ / utiliseWe use a dictionary.
💡
Study tip: These 10 verbs appear in over 50% of everyday English conversations. Master them first — everything else becomes much easier.

📝 2. Action Verbs vs State Verbs

Not all verbs work the same way. Action verbs describe things you physically do. State verbs describe situations, feelings, or mental states — and they are NOT normally used with -ing.

✅ Action Verbs — can use -ing

• run → She is running to the bus.
• eat → They are eating lunch.
• make → He is making dinner.
• go → I am going to the shop.

⚠️ State Verbs — do NOT use -ing

• know → ✅ "I know the answer." ❌ "I am knowing the answer."
• want → ✅ "She wants coffee." ❌ "She is wanting coffee."
• like → ✅ "He likes music." ❌ "He is liking music."
• have → ✅ "They have a car." ❌ "They are having a car."

🎯
Memory trick: State verbs describe things that don't change moment by moment — your knowledge, your feelings, your possessions. Because they don't change, they don't need the continuous (-ing) form.

⚠️ 3. Common Mistakes with Verbs

These are the most common verb errors that beginners make. Study them carefully!

❌ Common Errors

• ❌ "She is knowing the answer." — "know" is a state verb
• ❌ "I am wanting a coffee." — "want" is a state verb
• ❌ "He make dinner every day." — missing -s for he/she/it
• ❌ "They goes to school." — "go" doesn't add -es for they

✅ Correct Forms

• ✅ "She knows the answer."
• ✅ "I want a coffee."
• ✅ "He makes dinner every day."
• ✅ "They go to school."
