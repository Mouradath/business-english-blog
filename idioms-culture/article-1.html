What Are Idioms? A Simple Introduction

Discover what idioms are and why they are essential in everyday English.

← Back to Idioms Culture

What Is an Idiom?

An idiom is a phrase whose meaning is different from the literal meaning of its words. If you translate an idiom word by word, it often makes no sense!

"It's raining cats and dogs." (= it's raining very heavily)

"Break a leg!" (= good luck!)

"I'm feeling under the weather." (= I feel unwell)

Why Are Idioms Important?

Native English speakers use idioms constantly in conversation, films, books and news. Without knowing idioms, you may understand every word but still miss the meaning.

How to Learn Idioms

✔ Learn them in context, not in isolation

✔ Note when and how native speakers use them

✔ Watch films and TV shows to hear them naturally

✔ Practise using them in your own sentences

✓ What You Learned

Idioms are fixed expressions with non-literal meanings. They are everywhere in English. Learning them helps you understand real conversations and sound more natural.
