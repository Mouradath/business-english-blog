Proverbs & Their Meanings

Level: B1/B2 Idioms & Culture
0 / 11 points

1. What Is a Proverb?

A proverb is a short, well-known saying that expresses a general truth or piece of advice. Unlike idioms, proverbs are complete sentences — and they carry cultural wisdom passed down over generations.

"Actions speak louder than words."
Meaning: What you do matters more than what you say.

"Every cloud has a silver lining."
Meaning: There is something positive in every difficult situation.

Proverbs are used in everyday conversation, speeches, and writing to add weight and authority to a point. Recognising them makes you sound fluent and culturally aware.

3. Contradictory Proverbs

Interestingly, English has many proverbs that seem to contradict each other. This shows that proverbs are not universal truths — they are context-dependent pieces of wisdom.

Proverb AContradictsProverb B
"Look before you leap."vs"He who hesitates is lost."
"Many hands make light work."vs"Too many cooks spoil the broth."
"Absence makes the heart grow fonder."vs"Out of sight, out of mind."
"Good things come to those who wait."vs"The early bird catches the worm."
Key insight: The skill is knowing which proverb fits the situation — not memorising them as facts. Context determines which piece of wisdom applies.
