Prepositions Mastery

Level: A2/B1 Miscellaneous
0 / 11 points

1. Prepositions of Time: at, on, in

These three are the most commonly confused prepositions. The rule is simple once you see the pattern — they move from specific to general.

PrepositionUsed withExamples
atExact times, specific pointsat 9am, at noon, at midnight, at Christmas
onDays and dateson Monday, on 5 June, on my birthday
inMonths, years, seasons, longer periodsin June, in 2024, in winter, in the morning
Quick test: "The meeting is ___ Monday ___ 10am ___ the morning."
Answer: on Monday at 10am in the morning. ✅

2. Prepositions of Place: at, on, in

The same three prepositions also show location — again moving from a point to a surface to an enclosed space.

PrepositionUsed withExamples
atA specific point or locationat the door, at the station, at work, at home
onA surface or lineon the table, on the wall, on the bus, on the street
inAn enclosed space or areain the box, in the car, in London, in the garden

3. Tricky Preposition Combinations

Some prepositions follow fixed patterns with verbs and adjectives. These must be memorised — there is no logical rule.

ExpressionCorrect prepositionExample
interested ___inShe's interested in history.
good ___atHe's good at maths.
afraid ___ofI'm afraid of spiders.
depend ___onIt depends on the weather.
listen ___toListen to your teacher.
wait ___forI'm waiting for the bus.
different ___fromThis is different from that.
