Numbers, Dates & Time

Level: A2 Miscellaneous
0 / 11 points

1. Cardinal & Ordinal Numbers

Cardinal numbers count things (one, two, three…). Ordinal numbers show position or order (first, second, third…). Both are essential in everyday English.

CardinalOrdinalUsed for
1 / one1st / firstFloors, dates, rankings
2 / two2nd / secondPositions, chapters
3 / three3rd / thirdCompetitions, sequences
4–204th–20thAll use "-th" except 1st, 2nd, 3rd
21 / twenty-one21st / twenty-firstCompound ordinals follow same rules
Dates: We say "the fifth of June" or "June fifth" (US).
We write: 5 June 2025 (British) or June 5, 2025 (American).

2. Telling the Time

English has two common ways to tell the time — the 12-hour clock (with am/pm) and the 24-hour clock (used in transport and formal contexts).

WrittenSaid (formal)Said (informal)
9:00nine o'clocknine
9:15quarter past ninenine fifteen
9:30half past ninenine thirty
9:45quarter to tennine forty-five
13:00one o'clock (pm)thirteen hundred (military)
British vs American:
British: "half past seven" / "quarter to eight"
American: "seven thirty" / "seven forty-five"
