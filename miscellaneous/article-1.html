How to Start Learning English Today

Simple, practical steps to begin your English learning journey with confidence.

← Back to Miscellaneous

Getting Started Is the Hardest Part

Many people want to learn English but don't know where to begin. The good news: you don't need expensive courses or hours of study every day. Start small and build consistently.

Step 1: Set a Clear Goal

Why do you want to learn English? Travel? Work? Study? Social connections?

Be specific: "I want to have a basic conversation in English within 3 months."

Step 2: Learn the Most Common Words First

Focus on the 500 most common English words first.

These cover the majority of everyday conversations.

Use apps like Duolingo or Anki to learn them with spaced repetition.

Step 3: Listen Every Day

Listen to English for at least 15 minutes every day.

Start with slow, clear speech: BBC Learning English, Easy English News.

You don't need to understand everything — exposure builds familiarity.

Step 4: Speak from Day One

Don't wait until you're "ready" — start speaking early.

Talk to yourself in English. Describe your surroundings.

Find a language exchange partner or use apps like HelloTalk.

✓ What You Learned

Consistency beats intensity — 20 minutes daily is better than 3 hours once a week. Start with a clear goal, learn the most common words, listen every day, and speak from day one.
