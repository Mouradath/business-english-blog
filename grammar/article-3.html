Telling Stories in the Past

When we tell stories or talk about past events in English, we often use two tenses together: the Past Simple and the Past Continuous. Each one plays a different role in the story.

Past SimplePast Continuous
A completed actionAn action in progress
I walked to school.I was walking to school.
She called me.She was calling me.

Past Simple

We use the Past Simple for actions that started and finished in the past.

When to Use It

Completed actions

I watched a film last night.

She visited Paris in 2022.

They played football yesterday.

How to Form It

Regular verbs: verb + ed

walk → walked · work → worked · play → played

Irregular verbs must be memorised:

go → went · eat → ate · see → saw · buy → bought

Negative: I didn't watch / Question: Did you watch?

🕐 Signal Words for Past Simple

yesterday · last night · last week · in 2020 · ago · when I was young · once · then

Past Continuous

We use the Past Continuous for actions that were in progress at a specific moment in the past.

When to Use It

Action in progress at a past moment

At 8pm, I was watching TV.

She was sleeping when I called.

They were having dinner at that time.

How to Form It

Subject + was/were + verb-ing

I/He/She/It + was working

You/We/They + were working

Negative: I wasn't working / Question: Were you working?

Using Both Tenses Together

The most common use is when a longer background action (Past Continuous) is interrupted by a shorter completed action (Past Simple).

Background + Interruption

I was walking home when it started to rain.

She was cooking dinner when the phone rang.

They were watching TV when the lights went out.

💡 Key Words: When and While

When introduces the short completed action: "...when it started to rain."

While introduces the longer background action: "While I was walking home..."

Common Mistakes

❌ Wrong

I was walking to school yesterday.

While she cooked, the phone rang.

✅ Correct

I walked to school yesterday. (completed routine)

While she was cooking, the phone rang. (background action)

✅ What You Learned

Past Simple = completed actions. Past Continuous = actions in progress. Together they create vivid stories: the continuous sets the scene, the simple shows what happened.
