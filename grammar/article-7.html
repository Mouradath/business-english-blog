Reported Speech in Depth

Go beyond the basics and master complex reported speech structures.

What Is Reported Speech?

Reported speech (also called indirect speech) is how we tell someone what another person said — without using their exact words. When we report speech, the tense usually shifts back one step into the past.

Direct SpeechReported Speech
"I am tired."She said she was tired.
"I work in London."He said he worked in London.
"I have finished."She said she had finished.
"I will help you."He said he would help me.

Tense Backshift Rules

Tense changes in reported speech

Present Simple → Past Simple

Present Continuous → Past Continuous

Present Perfect → Past Perfect

Past Simple → Past Perfect

will → would · can → could · may → might

Reporting Questions

Yes/No Questions

"Are you coming?" → She asked if I was coming.

"Do you speak French?" → He asked whether I spoke French.

Wh- Questions

"Where do you live?" → She asked where I lived.

"What time is it?" → He asked what time it was.

💡 Key Rule for Reported Questions

In reported questions, use normal word order (not question order): ❌ She asked where did I live. ✅ She asked where I lived.

Reporting Commands and Requests

"Sit down!" → He told me to sit down.

"Please help me." → She asked me to help her.

"Don't be late!" → He told me not to be late.

When NOT to Backshift

If the situation is still true or current, you don't always need to backshift.

"The Earth orbits the Sun." → She said the Earth orbits the Sun. (still true)

"I love you." → He said he loves me. (feeling still exists)

✅ What You Learned

Reported speech shifts tenses back one step. Use "if/whether" for yes/no questions and wh-words for wh-questions. Commands use "tell/ask + to + verb". No backshift needed for permanent truths.
