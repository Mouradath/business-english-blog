Introduction to English Sentences

Learn the basic structure of English sentences: subject, verb, and object.

← Back to Grammar

What Is a Sentence?

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. Every English sentence must have at least two things: a subject and a verb.

Think of a sentence like a small story — someone does something. That someone is the subject, and what they do is the verb.

Examples

🐶 The dog runs.

👩 She smiles.

🌧️ It rains.

The Three Parts of a Sentence

Most English sentences follow this simple pattern:

1. Subject

The subject is who or what the sentence is about. It is usually a noun or pronoun.

Tom eats breakfast.

The cat sleeps.

They play football.

2. Verb

The verb is the action or state in the sentence. It tells us what the subject does or is.

Tom eats breakfast.

She is happy.

We love music.

3. Object

The object is who or what receives the action. Not all sentences need an object, but many do.

Tom eats breakfast.

She reads a book.

He drives a car.

Sentence Structure: Subject + Verb + Object

In English, the most common word order is S + V + O (Subject + Verb + Object). This is very important — English word order is quite fixed compared to many other languages.

S + V + O

I (S) + love (V) + coffee (O)

She (S) + watches (V) + TV (O)

The children (S) + play (V) + games (O)

💡 Key Tip

In English, the subject almost always comes BEFORE the verb. Unlike some languages, you cannot say "Eats Tom breakfast." The correct order is "Tom eats breakfast."

Types of Sentences

Positive Sentences

These sentences say that something IS true.

I like chocolate.

She works in a hospital.

Negative Sentences

These sentences say that something is NOT true. We use do not (don't) or does not (doesn't).

I don't like chocolate.

She doesn't work in a hospital.

Question Sentences

These sentences ask for information. We move the verb before the subject.

Do you like chocolate?

Does she work in a hospital?

✅ What You Learned

Every English sentence needs a subject and a verb. The basic order is Subject + Verb + Object. Positive, negative, and question sentences all follow clear patterns.
