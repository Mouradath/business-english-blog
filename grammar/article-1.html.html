Introduction to English Sentences

Learn the basic structure of English sentences: subject, verb, and object.

← Back to Grammar

What Is a Sentence?

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. Every English sentence must have at least two things: a subject and a verb.

Think of a sentence like a small story — someone does something. That someone is the subject, and what they do is the verb.

Examples

🐶 The dog runs.

👩 She smiles.

🌧️ It rains.

The Three Parts of a Sentence

1. Subject

The subject is who or what the sentence is about.

Tom eats breakfast.

The cat sleeps.

They play football.

2. Verb

The verb is the action or state in the sentence.

Tom eats breakfast.

She is happy.

We love music.

3. Object

The object is who or what receives the action.

Tom eats breakfast.

She reads a book.

He drives a car.

Positive, Negative and Question Sentences

Positive

I like chocolate.

Negative

I don't like chocolate.

Question

Do you like chocolate?

💡 Key Tip

In English, the subject almost always comes BEFORE the verb. The correct order is "Tom eats breakfast" — never "Eats Tom breakfast."

🧠 Quick Quiz

Test what you've learned — choose the best answer for each question!

1. Which part of a sentence tells us WHO or WHAT the sentence is about?

2. Which sentence is correct?

3. In the sentence "He drives a car", what is the OBJECT?

4. How do you make "I like coffee" into a negative sentence?

✅ What You Learned

Every English sentence needs a subject and a verb. The basic order is Subject + Verb + Object. Positive, negative and question sentences all follow clear patterns.
